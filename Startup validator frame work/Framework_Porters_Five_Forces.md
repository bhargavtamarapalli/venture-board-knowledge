# Porter's Five Forces Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Evaluate the attractiveness of an industry by analyzing the five competitive forces that influence profitability and long-term sustainability.

---

## When To Use

- Startup idea validation
- Market entry decisions
- Competitive strategy
- Investor due diligence
- Industry attractiveness assessment

---

## Inputs Required

- Target industry
- Customer segment
- Existing competitors
- Business model
- Revenue model

---

## Core Concepts

The five forces determine whether an industry is attractive for a new entrant.

A highly attractive market has:
- Healthy margins
- Limited competitive pressure
- Sustainable differentiation

---

# The Five Forces

## 1. Threat of New Entrants

Evaluate:
- Capital requirements
- Brand loyalty
- Distribution barriers
- Technology barriers
- Regulatory barriers

Higher barriers = More attractive market.

---

## 2. Bargaining Power of Suppliers

Evaluate:
- Number of suppliers
- Switching costs
- Supplier concentration
- Dependency risk

Lower supplier power is preferred.

---

## 3. Bargaining Power of Buyers

Evaluate:
- Customer concentration
- Price sensitivity
- Switching costs
- Availability of alternatives

Lower buyer power increases pricing flexibility.

---

## 4. Threat of Substitutes

Evaluate:
- Alternative solutions
- Ease of switching
- Relative price-performance
- Customer loyalty

High substitute pressure reduces long-term profitability.

---

## 5. Competitive Rivalry

Evaluate:
- Number of competitors
- Market growth
- Product differentiation
- Price competition
- Marketing intensity

Lower rivalry generally improves profitability.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Are entry barriers defensible?
- Can suppliers dictate pricing?
- Can customers easily switch?
- Are substitutes abundant?
- Is differentiation sustainable?

---

## Questions To Ask

- Why hasn't a dominant player already won?
- What prevents a new competitor from copying the idea?
- What creates pricing power?
- Which force poses the greatest risk?

---

## Metrics

- Market concentration
- Gross margin
- Customer switching cost
- Supplier dependency
- Competitive intensity
- Market growth rate

---

## Red Flags

- Commodity market
- Zero switching costs
- Intense price wars
- Dominant incumbent
- Low barriers to entry

---

## Best Practices

- Evaluate each force independently.
- Support findings with evidence.
- Reassess after significant market changes.
- Combine with SWOT and Moat Analysis.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Ignoring substitute products.
- Confusing competitors with substitutes.
- Assuming market growth guarantees profitability.
- Focusing only on direct competitors.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Five Forces Assessment Table
- Force Rating (Low / Medium / High)
- Industry Attractiveness Score
- Key Risks
- Strategic Recommendations
- Overall Conclusion
