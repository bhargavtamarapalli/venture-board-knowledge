# Jobs To Be Done (JTBD)

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Evaluate whether a product solves a meaningful customer job instead of merely offering features.

---

## When To Use

- Idea validation
- Product design
- Customer research
- Feature prioritization
- Positioning

---

## Inputs Required

- Target customer
- Customer context
- Existing alternatives
- Desired outcome

---

## Core Concepts

Customers "hire" products to accomplish a job.

A job has:
- Functional needs
- Emotional needs
- Social needs

Focus on the underlying job rather than requested features.

---

## JTBD Framework

### Functional Job
What task is the customer trying to complete?

### Emotional Job
How should the customer feel?

### Social Job
How does the customer want to be perceived?

### Current Solution
How is the job solved today?

### Desired Outcome
What would success look like?

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Is the job frequent?
- Is the job painful?
- Are existing solutions inadequate?
- Will customers switch?
- Can the solution be differentiated?

---

## Questions To Ask

- What triggers this job?
- What alternatives exist?
- Why are they unsatisfied?
- What outcome matters most?
- What prevents switching?

---

## Metrics

- Problem Frequency
- Problem Severity
- Time Saved
- Cost Saved
- Adoption Rate
- Retention
- NPS

---

## Red Flags

- Solving an infrequent job
- Nice-to-have instead of must-have
- Customers cannot describe the pain
- Weak switching incentive

---

## Best Practices

- Interview customers before building.
- Focus on outcomes, not features.
- Validate willingness to pay.
- Prioritize high-frequency, high-impact jobs.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Asking customers what features they want.
- Defining customers too broadly.
- Ignoring emotional motivations.
- Confusing user tasks with business goals.

---

## Output Format

Produce:
- Primary Job
- Functional Job
- Emotional Job
- Social Job
- Current Alternatives
- Desired Outcomes
- Opportunity Assessment
- Validation Recommendations
