# Pricing Strategy Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Develop a pricing strategy that maximizes customer value, profitability, competitive positioning and long-term business growth. This framework helps founders determine what to charge, how to monetize and when to evolve pricing.

---

## When To Use

- Startup idea validation
- Product launch
- Pricing optimization
- Investor due diligence
- Revenue planning
- Business model evaluation

---

## Inputs Required

- Value proposition
- Target customer
- Customer willingness-to-pay research
- Competitor pricing
- Cost structure
- Unit economics
- Revenue goals

---

## Core Concepts

Pricing is a strategic decision based on customer value rather than development cost.

An effective pricing strategy should:

- Reflect delivered value
- Support sustainable margins
- Be easy to understand
- Scale with customer success
- Differentiate from competitors

Common pricing models include:

- Subscription
- Usage-based
- Freemium
- Tiered
- One-time purchase
- Enterprise contracts

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Define Pricing Objectives

Determine whether the priority is:

- Growth
- Profitability
- Market penetration
- Premium positioning
- Customer expansion

---

### Step 2 — Evaluate Customer Value

Estimate measurable customer outcomes including:

- Revenue growth
- Cost savings
- Productivity gains
- Risk reduction

---

### Step 3 — Analyze Competitors

Compare:

- Pricing models
- Feature tiers
- Billing frequency
- Enterprise offerings
- Free plans

---

### Step 4 — Validate Pricing

Use:

- Customer interviews
- Paid pilots
- A/B pricing experiments
- Pre-orders
- Sales conversations

---

### Step 5 — Optimize

Review pricing performance regularly using business metrics and customer feedback.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Pricing aligned with value
- Customer willingness validated
- Healthy gross margins
- Unit economics sustainable
- Pricing easy to understand
- Competitive differentiation maintained

---

## Questions To Ask

- Why will customers pay?
- What outcome justifies the price?
- Which pricing model fits customer buying behavior?
- How will pricing evolve over time?
- Does pricing support long-term profitability?

---

## Metrics

- ARPU
- MRR
- ARR
- Gross Margin
- CAC
- LTV
- LTV:CAC Ratio
- Conversion Rate
- Expansion Revenue

---

## Red Flags

- Cost-plus pricing only
- Unsustainable discounts
- Weak unit economics
- Excessive pricing complexity
- No pricing validation

---

## Best Practices

- Price according to customer value.
- Validate pricing before scaling.
- Review pricing periodically.
- Minimize pricing complexity.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Competing only on price.
- Ignoring willingness to pay.
- Underpricing premium value.
- Offering too many pricing tiers.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Pricing Strategy
- Pricing Model
- Recommended Price Range
- Value Justification
- Financial Impact
- Risks
- Recommendations
