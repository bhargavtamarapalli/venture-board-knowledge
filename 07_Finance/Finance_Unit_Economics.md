# Unit Economics Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Evaluate whether each customer acquired generates sustainable long-term profitability. Unit economics measure the financial viability of the business model by comparing customer acquisition costs with customer lifetime value and contribution margins.

---

## When To Use

- Startup idea validation
- Investor due diligence
- Pricing strategy
- Financial planning
- Go-To-Market optimization
- Growth planning

---

## Inputs Required

- Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
- Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)
- Pricing model
- Gross Margin
- Churn Rate
- Retention data
- Revenue metrics

---

## Core Concepts

Unit economics determine whether growth creates value or destroys value.

Every additional customer should contribute positive long-term profit after acquisition and servicing costs.

Core metrics include:

- Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
- Lifetime Value (LTV)
- LTV:CAC Ratio
- Gross Margin
- Contribution Margin
- CAC Payback Period

Healthy unit economics indicate scalable growth.

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Calculate Revenue Per Customer

Determine recurring revenue generated by an average customer.

---

### Step 2 — Calculate Customer Acquisition Cost

Include:

- Marketing
- Sales
- Promotions
- Partner commissions

---

### Step 3 — Calculate Gross Margin

Subtract direct service delivery costs from revenue.

---

### Step 4 — Calculate Lifetime Value

Estimate customer value using retention, revenue and gross margin.

---

### Step 5 — Evaluate Efficiency

Measure:

- LTV:CAC Ratio
- CAC Payback
- Contribution Margin
- Break-even Period

---

### Step 6 — Improve Economics

Optimize pricing, retention, acquisition efficiency and operational costs.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Positive contribution margin
- Sustainable CAC
- Healthy LTV:CAC ratio
- Acceptable payback period
- Strong retention
- Scalable business model

---

## Questions To Ask

- Does every customer generate profit?
- How quickly is CAC recovered?
- Which acquisition channels are most efficient?
- Can margins improve over time?
- What assumptions influence LTV?

---

## Metrics

- CAC
- LTV
- LTV:CAC Ratio
- Gross Margin
- Contribution Margin
- CAC Payback Period
- ARPU
- Churn Rate

---

## Red Flags

- LTV lower than CAC
- Negative contribution margin
- Long payback period
- Weak retention
- Rising acquisition costs

---

## Best Practices

- Review unit economics monthly.
- Improve retention before increasing acquisition.
- Measure channel-level economics.
- Validate assumptions continuously.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Ignoring servicing costs.
- Using unrealistic retention assumptions.
- Measuring revenue without margins.
- Scaling before profitability.
