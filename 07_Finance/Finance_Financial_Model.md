# Financial Model Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Develop a structured financial model to evaluate the economic viability, funding requirements, profitability and scalability of a startup. The model supports investment decisions by translating business assumptions into measurable financial outcomes.

---

## When To Use

- Startup idea validation
- Investor due diligence
- Fundraising
- Budget planning
- Scenario analysis
- Strategic planning

---

## Inputs Required

- Business model
- Pricing strategy
- Customer acquisition assumptions
- Revenue projections
- Cost structure
- Hiring plan
- Capital expenditure
- Funding assumptions

---

## Core Concepts

A financial model converts business assumptions into projected financial statements.

The objective is to understand:

- Revenue potential
- Cost structure
- Cash flow
- Profitability
- Funding requirements
- Break-even timeline

Use conservative assumptions and clearly distinguish facts from estimates.

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Revenue Forecast

Estimate revenue using:

- Customer growth
- Pricing
- Conversion rates
- Retention
- Expansion revenue

---

### Step 2 — Cost Forecast

Estimate:

- Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
- Operating Expenses
- Sales & Marketing
- Research & Development
- General & Administrative

---

### Step 3 — Cash Flow

Project:

- Operating Cash Flow
- Investing Cash Flow
- Financing Cash Flow
- Cash Runway

---

### Step 4 — Profitability

Calculate:

- Gross Profit
- EBITDA
- Net Profit
- Break-even Point

---

### Step 5 — Scenario Analysis

Prepare:

- Base Case
- Best Case
- Worst Case

Evaluate the impact of changing key assumptions.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Revenue assumptions documented
- Cost assumptions realistic
- Cash runway calculated
- Break-even estimated
- Funding requirements identified
- Scenarios analyzed

---

## Questions To Ask

- What assumptions drive revenue?
- Which costs scale with growth?
- When does the business become profitable?
- How much funding is required?
- What risks could materially impact projections?

---

## Metrics

- Revenue
- Gross Margin
- EBITDA
- Net Margin
- Burn Rate
- Cash Runway
- Break-even Month
- Operating Cash Flow

---

## Red Flags

- Unrealistic revenue growth
- Missing cost categories
- No scenario planning
- Short cash runway
- Funding assumptions unsupported

---

## Best Practices

- Build bottom-up forecasts.
- Update assumptions regularly.
- Separate assumptions from results.
- Validate against market benchmarks.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Overestimating revenue.
- Underestimating operating costs.
- Ignoring working capital.
- Presenting a single forecast only.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Financial Summary
- Revenue Forecast
- Cost Forecast
- Cash Flow Projection
- Scenario Analysis
- Funding Requirements
- Risks
- Recommendations
