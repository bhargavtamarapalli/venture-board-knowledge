# Business Model Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Provide a structured methodology to evaluate how a startup creates, delivers and captures value. A strong business model demonstrates long-term sustainability, profitability and scalability while aligning customer value with business objectives.

---

## When To Use

- Startup idea validation
- Business planning
- Investor due diligence
- Revenue strategy
- Product strategy
- Go-To-Market planning

---

## Inputs Required

- Value proposition
- Target customer segment
- Pricing strategy
- Distribution channels
- Cost structure
- Competitive analysis
- Financial assumptions

---

## Core Concepts

A business model explains how an organization generates sustainable value.

It answers:

- Who is the customer?
- What value is delivered?
- How is value delivered?
- How does the company earn revenue?
- What are the major costs?
- Why is the model defensible?

A strong model balances customer value with profitable economics.

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Customer Segments

Define primary and secondary customer groups.

### Step 2 — Value Proposition

Describe the unique value delivered to each segment.

### Step 3 — Revenue Streams

Identify monetization methods such as:
- Subscription
- Transaction fees
- Usage-based pricing
- Licensing
- Advertising
- Marketplace commissions

### Step 4 — Cost Structure

Document fixed and variable costs including product development, infrastructure, sales, marketing and operations.

### Step 5 — Key Resources & Activities

Identify the critical assets, capabilities and activities required for execution.

### Step 6 — Scalability Assessment

Evaluate operational leverage, recurring revenue potential and expansion opportunities.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Customer segment validated
- Value proposition differentiated
- Revenue model defined
- Cost structure realistic
- Scalable operations identified
- Sustainable competitive advantage considered

---

## Questions To Ask

- How does the business make money?
- Why will customers pay?
- Which revenue stream is most profitable?
- What are the largest costs?
- Can the model scale efficiently?

---

## Metrics

- Revenue Growth
- Gross Margin
- Contribution Margin
- CAC
- LTV
- Payback Period
- Operating Margin
- Burn Multiple

---

## Red Flags

- No clear monetization strategy
- High operating costs
- Weak gross margins
- Revenue dependent on one customer
- Poor scalability

---

## Best Practices

- Keep revenue models simple.
- Prioritize recurring revenue.
- Validate willingness to pay.
- Review unit economics regularly.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Confusing product with business model.
- Underestimating operating costs.
- Ignoring scalability.
- Overcomplicating pricing.

---

## Output Format

- Business Model Summary

- Scalability Assessment

- Recommendations
