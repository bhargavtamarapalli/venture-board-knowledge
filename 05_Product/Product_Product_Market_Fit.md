# Product-Market Fit Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Evaluate whether a product consistently solves an important customer problem for a clearly defined market, resulting in sustainable adoption, retention and growth.

---

## When To Use

- Startup idea validation
- MVP evaluation
- Product strategy
- Investor due diligence
- Growth planning
- Scale readiness

---

## Inputs Required

- Customer interviews
- Product usage analytics
- Retention data
- Churn analysis
- NPS / satisfaction surveys
- Revenue metrics

---

## Core Concepts

Product-Market Fit (PMF) exists when a well-defined customer segment repeatedly chooses, adopts and recommends a product because it delivers meaningful value.

PMF is demonstrated through customer behavior rather than founder belief.

Key indicators include:
- High retention
- Organic referrals
- Strong engagement
- Repeat purchases
- Sustainable growth

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Validate Problem
Confirm the problem is frequent, painful and valuable.

### Step 2 — Validate Solution
Ensure customers successfully achieve their desired outcome.

### Step 3 — Measure Adoption
Track activation, engagement and repeat usage.

### Step 4 — Measure Retention
Evaluate customer retention and churn over time.

### Step 5 — Measure Advocacy
Assess referrals, reviews and customer satisfaction.

### Step 6 — Iterate
Improve the product using evidence until PMF indicators consistently improve.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- ICP clearly defined
- Problem validated
- Customers receive measurable value
- Retention increasing
- Churn understood
- Positive word-of-mouth observed

---

## Questions To Ask

- Would customers be disappointed if the product disappeared?
- Why do customers stay?
- Why do customers leave?
- Which customer segment shows the strongest adoption?
- What blocks broader adoption?

---

## Metrics

- Activation Rate
- DAU / MAU
- Retention Rate
- Churn Rate
- NPS
- Referral Rate
- Expansion Revenue
- Net Revenue Retention

---

## Red Flags

- High churn
- Low engagement
- Weak retention
- Customers cannot explain the value
- Growth depends entirely on paid acquisition

---

## Best Practices

- Focus on one customer segment.
- Measure customer behavior instead of opinions.
- Iterate quickly using feedback.
- Delay scaling until PMF signals are strong.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Declaring PMF too early.
- Measuring vanity metrics.
- Expanding before retention.
- Ignoring churn reasons.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- PMF Assessment
- Evidence Summary
- KPI Dashboard
- Strengths
- Risks
- Recommendations
