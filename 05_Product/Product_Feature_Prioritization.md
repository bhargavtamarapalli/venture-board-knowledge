# Product Feature Prioritization Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Provide a structured process for selecting which product features should be built first based on customer value, business impact, implementation effort, strategic alignment and evidence. The objective is to maximize learning and business outcomes while minimizing wasted development effort.

---

## When To Use

- MVP planning
- Product roadmap development
- Sprint planning
- Product-market fit validation
- Resource allocation
- Investor due diligence

---

## Inputs Required

- Product vision
- Customer interviews
- Jobs To Be Done
- Feature backlog
- Business objectives
- Engineering estimates
- Market research

---

## Core Concepts

Every feature competes for limited engineering resources.

Prioritization should be evidence-driven rather than opinion-driven.

Evaluate features using:

- Customer value
- Business value
- Strategic alignment
- Technical effort
- Risk reduction
- Learning potential

Combine this framework with MoSCoW, RICE and Kano for quantitative prioritization.

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Build Feature Backlog

List all proposed features.

---

### Step 2 — Define Evaluation Criteria

Evaluate:

- Customer Impact
- Revenue Impact
- Strategic Importance
- Technical Complexity
- Development Effort
- Risk Reduction

---

### Step 3 — Score Features

Assign scores for each criterion.

Document assumptions.

---

### Step 4 — Rank Features

Group into:

- Immediate
- Next Release
- Future
- Reject

---

### Step 5 — Review

Validate priorities using customer evidence and business objectives.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Customer problem validated
- Feature supports product vision
- Evidence available
- Engineering effort estimated
- Business impact significant
- Dependencies identified

---

## Questions To Ask

- Does this solve a critical customer problem?
- Will it improve retention?
- Can it wait?
- What assumption does it validate?
- Is there a simpler solution?

---

## Metrics

- Customer Impact
- Revenue Impact
- Development Effort
- RICE Score
- Kano Category
- Adoption Rate
- Feature Usage

---

## Red Flags

- Building features without evidence
- Executive opinion overrides data
- Large low-value features
- Feature creep
- Ignoring opportunity cost

---

## Best Practices

- Prioritize customer outcomes.
- Review priorities regularly.
- Keep the backlog small.
- Remove low-value features aggressively.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Prioritizing by loudest stakeholder.
- Ignoring engineering effort.
- Measuring success by shipped features.
- Building for edge cases.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Prioritized Feature List
- Scoring Matrix
- MVP Features
- Deferred Features
- Risks
- Recommendations
