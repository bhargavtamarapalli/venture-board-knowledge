# Product Roadmap Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Define a strategic roadmap that aligns product development with customer needs, business objectives and measurable outcomes. The roadmap should communicate priorities, sequencing and expected value rather than acting as a fixed feature schedule.

---

## When To Use

- Product strategy
- MVP planning
- Quarterly planning
- Investor due diligence
- Team alignment
- Resource planning

---

## Inputs Required

- Product vision
- MVP definition
- Feature prioritization
- Customer research
- Business objectives
- Engineering capacity

---

## Core Concepts

A roadmap is a strategic communication tool, not a promise.

It should be outcome-driven rather than feature-driven.

Each roadmap item should contribute to:
- Customer value
- Business growth
- Product-Market Fit
- Risk reduction

Organize work into:
- Now
- Next
- Later

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Define Product Vision

State the long-term product objective.

### Step 2 — Identify Strategic Themes

Examples:
- Customer Acquisition
- Activation
- Retention
- Monetization
- Platform Scalability

### Step 3 — Prioritize Initiatives

Use evidence from:
- Customer interviews
- Product metrics
- Feature prioritization
- Business strategy

### Step 4 — Sequence Work

Plan initiatives by dependency, impact and effort.

### Step 5 — Define Success Metrics

Assign measurable outcomes for each initiative.

### Step 6 — Review Regularly

Update the roadmap using customer feedback and business learning.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Vision clearly defined
- Strategic themes identified
- Priorities evidence-based
- Dependencies documented
- Outcomes measurable
- Resources realistic

---

## Questions To Ask

- Does this initiative support the vision?
- What customer problem is solved?
- What business outcome is expected?
- Can lower-value work be removed?
- What assumptions remain?

---

## Metrics

- Roadmap Completion
- Feature Adoption
- Activation Rate
- Retention Rate
- Revenue Growth
- Customer Satisfaction
- Time to Value

---

## Red Flags

- Feature factory mindset
- No prioritization rationale
- Too many parallel initiatives
- Fixed long-term commitments
- Roadmap disconnected from customer evidence

---

## Best Practices

- Focus on outcomes over outputs.
- Keep the roadmap flexible.
- Review quarterly.
- Communicate priorities clearly.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Treating roadmap dates as guarantees.
- Prioritizing internal requests over customer value.
- Ignoring dependencies.
- Updating the roadmap too infrequently.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Product Vision
- Strategic Themes
- Now / Next / Later Roadmap
- Success Metrics
- Risks
- Recommendations
