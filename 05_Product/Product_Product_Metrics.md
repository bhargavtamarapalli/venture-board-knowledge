# Product Metrics Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Define the key product metrics required to evaluate customer value, product adoption, retention, growth and business performance. This framework ensures product decisions are driven by measurable outcomes rather than intuition.

---

## When To Use

- Product strategy
- MVP evaluation
- Product-Market Fit assessment
- Growth optimization
- Investor due diligence
- Executive reporting

---

## Inputs Required

- Product analytics
- Customer interviews
- Business objectives
- Revenue metrics
- Usage data
- Customer feedback

---

## Core Concepts

Product metrics should measure outcomes, not activity.

A balanced product dashboard should include:

- Acquisition
- Activation
- Engagement
- Retention
- Revenue
- Referral

Prioritize actionable metrics over vanity metrics.

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Define Business Objectives

Identify the primary outcome the product should achieve.

### Step 2 — Select North Star Metric

Choose one metric representing long-term customer value.

### Step 3 — Define Supporting Metrics

Track:

- Acquisition
- Activation
- Engagement
- Retention
- Monetization
- Referral

### Step 4 — Establish Targets

Set measurable goals and review cadence.

### Step 5 — Review and Improve

Use metric trends to prioritize product improvements.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- North Star Metric defined
- Metrics aligned with business goals
- Customer outcomes measurable
- Leading and lagging indicators included
- Targets documented
- Review cadence established

---

## Questions To Ask

- What metric best represents customer success?
- Which metric predicts retention?
- Which feature drives engagement?
- Where are customers dropping off?
- Which metrics influence revenue?

---

## Metrics

- North Star Metric
- DAU
- WAU
- MAU
- Activation Rate
- Feature Adoption
- Retention Rate
- Churn Rate
- NPS
- ARPU
- LTV
- CAC
- Net Revenue Retention

---

## Red Flags

- Vanity metrics
- Too many KPIs
- Metrics without targets
- No retention tracking
- No customer outcome measurement

---

## Best Practices

- Track trends instead of isolated numbers.
- Use a small set of actionable KPIs.
- Review metrics regularly.
- Connect every metric to a business objective.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Measuring outputs instead of outcomes.
- Ignoring retention.
- Tracking everything equally.
- Optimizing one metric at the expense of customer value.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Product KPI Dashboard
- North Star Metric
- Supporting KPIs
- Trend Analysis
- Risks
- Recommendations
