# Validation Playbook Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Provide a repeatable process for validating startup ideas using evidence rather than assumptions. The playbook defines experiments, success criteria, decision rules and documentation standards to systematically reduce business risk before scaling.

---

## When To Use

- Startup idea validation
- MVP development
- Product-Market Fit validation
- Investor due diligence
- Go-To-Market planning
- New feature validation

---

## Inputs Required

- Business hypothesis
- Customer assumptions
- Product assumptions
- Market research
- Customer interview data
- Existing validation results

---

## Core Concepts

Validation is a continuous learning process.

The objective is to replace uncertainty with measurable evidence through low-cost, fast experiments.

Every experiment should include:

- Hypothesis
- Experiment
- Success Criteria
- Evidence
- Decision
- Next Action

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Define Hypothesis

Document the assumption to be validated.

---

### Step 2 — Prioritize

Rank hypotheses by:

- Business impact
- Uncertainty
- Cost of failure

---

### Step 3 — Select Experiment

Possible experiments include:

- Customer interviews
- Landing pages
- Concierge MVP
- Smoke tests
- Prototype testing
- Paid campaigns
- Pre-orders

---

### Step 4 — Execute

Collect objective evidence.

Record observations and outcomes.

---

### Step 5 — Evaluate

Compare results with predefined success criteria.

Classify as:

- Validated
- Partially Validated
- Invalidated

---

### Step 6 — Decide

Choose to:

- Continue
- Pivot
- Stop

Document reasoning.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Hypothesis documented
- Success criteria measurable
- Experiment low-cost
- Evidence recorded
- Decision justified
- Learnings shared

---

## Questions To Ask

- What assumption is being tested?
- What evidence is required?
- What result invalidates the hypothesis?
- What is the fastest experiment?
- What decision depends on this outcome?

---

## Metrics

- Validation Rate
- Experiment Cycle Time
- Learning Velocity
- Cost per Experiment
- Assumptions Eliminated
- Evidence Quality

---

## Red Flags

- No predefined success criteria
- Confirmation bias
- Large experiments before validation
- Ignoring failed experiments
- Poor documentation

---

## Best Practices

- Run inexpensive experiments first.
- Measure customer behavior.
- Define success before testing.
- Maintain a validation log.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Testing multiple hypotheses together.
- Measuring opinions instead of actions.
- Scaling before validation.
- Treating validation as a one-time activity.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Validation Plan
- Experiment Backlog
- Evidence Summary
- Decisions
- Risks
- Recommendations
