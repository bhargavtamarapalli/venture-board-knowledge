# Startup Failure Patterns Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Identify recurring startup failure patterns before they become existential risks. This framework enables founders and investors to proactively recognize warning signs, challenge assumptions, and implement corrective actions based on historical evidence.

---

## When To Use

- Startup idea validation
- Investor due diligence
- Product strategy reviews
- Quarterly business reviews
- Fundraising readiness
- Strategic planning

---

## Inputs Required

- Business model
- Customer validation
- Market research
- Financial model
- Product roadmap
- Team assessment
- Competitive analysis

---

## Core Concepts

Most startup failures are caused by predictable execution failures rather than bad ideas.

Common failure categories include:

- No Product-Market Fit
- Weak Customer Validation
- Poor Unit Economics
- Premature Scaling
- Founder Misalignment
- Cash Flow Problems
- Weak Go-To-Market Execution
- Competitive Pressure
- Regulatory Challenges
- Technical Debt

The objective is early detection and mitigation.

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Identify Failure Signals

Evaluate each major business function for early warning signs.

### Step 2 — Assess Severity

Classify every failure pattern as:

- Low
- Medium
- High
- Critical

---

### Step 3 — Determine Root Cause

Separate symptoms from underlying business problems.

---

### Step 4 — Define Corrective Actions

Recommend measurable actions to reduce or eliminate each failure pattern.

---

### Step 5 — Monitor Continuously

Review failure indicators regularly and update mitigation plans.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- PMF validated
- Customer evidence available
- Unit economics sustainable
- Cash runway sufficient
- Team execution capability assessed
- Competitive risks monitored

---

## Questions To Ask

- What assumption is most likely to fail?
- What could cause the company to run out of cash?
- Which customer signal is deteriorating?
- Are we scaling too early?
- Which execution risks remain unresolved?

---

## Metrics

- Burn Rate
- Runway
- Churn Rate
- Retention
- CAC
- LTV
- Revenue Growth
- PMF Indicators

---

## Red Flags

- Growing without retention
- Weak customer validation
- High burn with slow learning
- Founder disagreement
- Declining customer engagement

---

## Best Practices

- Learn from historical startup failures.
- Validate assumptions continuously.
- Focus on execution discipline.
- Review failure patterns quarterly.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Ignoring weak signals.
- Scaling before validation.
- Optimizing vanity metrics.
- Delaying difficult decisions.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Failure Pattern Assessment
- Root Cause Analysis
- Risk Severity
- Corrective Actions
- Monitoring Plan
- Recommendations
