# Kill Criteria Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Define objective conditions under which a startup idea, product initiative, or strategic direction should be stopped, pivoted or fundamentally redesigned. The framework minimizes sunk-cost bias by replacing emotional decisions with evidence-based decision rules.

---

## When To Use

- Startup idea validation
- MVP evaluation
- Product-Market Fit assessment
- Investor due diligence
- Quarterly business reviews
- Major strategic decisions

---

## Inputs Required

- Validation results
- Customer interview evidence
- Product metrics
- Financial model
- Unit economics
- Market research
- Risk assessment

---

## Core Concepts

Every startup should establish predefined kill criteria before committing significant resources.

Kill criteria should be:

- Objective
- Measurable
- Time-bound
- Evidence-based
- Agreed upon before execution

Possible outcomes:

- Continue
- Pivot
- Pause
- Terminate

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Define Critical Assumptions

Identify assumptions whose failure would invalidate the business.

---

### Step 2 — Establish Decision Thresholds

Examples:

- Minimum customer interview validation
- Maximum acceptable churn
- Minimum willingness to pay
- Required retention level
- Maximum CAC
- Minimum LTV:CAC ratio
- Required runway

---

### Step 3 — Monitor Performance

Measure progress against predefined thresholds using reliable data.

---

### Step 4 — Evaluate Outcomes

Classify each criterion as:

- Passed
- At Risk
- Failed

---

### Step 5 — Decide

Choose one action:

- Continue
- Iterate
- Pivot
- Kill

Document evidence supporting the decision.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Kill criteria documented
- Thresholds measurable
- Evidence collected
- Decisions unbiased
- Stakeholders aligned
- Review schedule established

---

## Questions To Ask

- What evidence would invalidate this business?
- Which assumptions remain unproven?
- Are we continuing because of evidence or sunk cost?
- What metrics define success?
- What conditions require a pivot?

---

## Metrics

- Validation Rate
- Retention
- Churn
- CAC
- LTV:CAC
- Burn Rate
- Runway
- Customer Satisfaction

---

## Red Flags

- No exit criteria
- Emotional decision-making
- Ignoring negative evidence
- Constantly changing success metrics
- Escalating investment without validation

---

## Best Practices

- Define kill criteria before execution.
- Use quantitative thresholds whenever possible.
- Review decisions objectively.
- Encourage independent Red Team review.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Moving goalposts after poor results.
- Ignoring contradictory evidence.
- Delaying difficult decisions.
- Continuing because of sunk costs.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Kill Criteria Register
- Current Status
- Evidence Summary
- Recommendation
- Continue / Pivot / Kill Decision
- Rationale
