# Buyer Personas Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Create evidence-based buyer personas that represent the decision-makers, influencers, users and economic buyers involved in purchasing the product. Buyer personas help align product development, marketing, sales and customer success around real customer needs.

---

## When To Use

- Startup idea validation
- Product design
- Marketing campaigns
- Sales enablement
- Go-To-Market planning
- Investor due diligence

---

## Inputs Required

- Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
- Customer interviews
- Sales conversations
- Product usage data (if available)
- Market research
- Competitor insights

---

## Core Concepts

A buyer persona is a research-backed representation of a specific customer type.

Personas should describe:
- Goals
- Responsibilities
- Pain points
- Buying motivations
- Decision criteria
- Objections
- Preferred communication channels

Differentiate between:
- Economic Buyer
- Decision Maker
- End User
- Influencer
- Champion

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Identify Persona Types

List every stakeholder involved in purchasing.

### Step 2 — Define Demographics

Capture:
- Role
- Industry
- Company size
- Experience
- Geography

### Step 3 — Define Psychographics

Capture:
- Goals
- Frustrations
- Success metrics
- Motivations
- Risk tolerance

### Step 4 — Buying Behaviour

Document:
- Buying triggers
- Research process
- Evaluation criteria
- Budget ownership
- Purchase objections

### Step 5 — Validate

Validate personas using customer interviews and observed behavior rather than assumptions.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Persona supported by evidence
- Goals clearly defined
- Pain points validated
- Buying authority identified
- Decision criteria documented
- Marketing channels identified

---

## Questions To Ask

- What does success look like?
- What problems consume the most time?
- What causes them to buy?
- What objections delay purchasing?
- Who influences the decision?

---

## Metrics

- Conversion Rate
- Sales Cycle Length
- Customer Acquisition Cost
- Lifetime Value
- Retention Rate
- Persona-specific Win Rate

---

## Red Flags

- Fictional personas
- Demographics without behaviors
- No interview validation
- Too many personas
- Generic descriptions

---

## Best Practices

- Build personas from interview evidence.
- Prioritize the primary buyer.
- Update personas as markets evolve.
- Separate ICP from personas.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Creating aspirational personas.
- Ignoring decision-makers.
- Confusing users with buyers.
- Overcomplicating persona documents.

---

## Output Format

Produce:
- Persona Summary
- Goals
- Pain Points
- Buying Journey
- Decision Criteria
- Objections
- Messaging Recommendations
