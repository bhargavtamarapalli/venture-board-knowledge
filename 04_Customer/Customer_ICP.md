# Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Define the ideal customer segment most likely to receive significant value from the product while generating sustainable revenue and long-term business growth. A well-defined ICP improves product decisions, marketing efficiency, sales effectiveness and customer retention.

---

## When To Use

- Startup idea validation
- Customer research
- Product strategy
- Go-To-Market planning
- Sales planning
- Investor due diligence

---

## Inputs Required

- Problem statement
- Customer interviews
- Market research
- Competitor analysis
- Existing customer data (if available)
- Product capabilities

---

## Core Concepts

An Ideal Customer Profile describes the organization or individual that gains the highest value from the solution and is most likely to purchase, adopt and remain a long-term customer.

An ICP is not "everyone."

A strong ICP defines:

- Who experiences the problem most frequently
- Who has budget
- Who has purchasing authority
- Who gains measurable value
- Who is easiest to acquire and retain

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Identify the Problem

Define the customer's highest-priority problem.

### Step 2 — Segment the Market

Segment by:

- Industry
- Company size
- Geography
- Revenue
- Team size
- Customer type
- Use case

### Step 3 — Define Characteristics

Capture:

- Goals
- Pain points
- Buying triggers
- Budget
- Decision maker
- Technical maturity

### Step 4 — Evaluate Fit

Rate each segment on:

- Problem severity
- Willingness to pay
- Market size
- Ease of acquisition
- Retention potential

### Step 5 — Prioritize

Select the highest-value customer segment for the initial GTM strategy.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Specific customer defined
- Problem validated
- Buying authority identified
- Budget exists
- Clear acquisition channels
- Segment large enough to support growth

---

## Questions To Ask

- Who experiences the problem most often?
- Who pays today?
- Who makes the buying decision?
- Why would they switch?
- Which segment delivers the highest lifetime value?

---

## Metrics

- CAC
- LTV
- Conversion Rate
- Retention Rate
- Churn Rate
- Average Revenue per Customer
- Sales Cycle Length

---

## Red Flags

- Targeting everyone
- No clear buyer
- No validated pain
- Low willingness to pay
- Small addressable segment

---

## Best Practices

- Base the ICP on customer evidence.
- Revisit after product-market fit improves.
- Separate ICP from buyer personas.
- Focus on one beachhead segment initially.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Confusing users with buyers.
- Creating overly broad segments.
- Ignoring budget and authority.
- Building personas before defining the ICP.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- ICP Summary
- Customer Characteristics
- Pain Points
- Buying Triggers
- Qualification Criteria
- Risks
- Recommendations
