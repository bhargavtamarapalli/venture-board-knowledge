# Customer Willingness To Pay Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Determine whether target customers are willing to pay for the proposed solution, how much they are willing to pay, and which pricing model best aligns with perceived customer value.

---

## When To Use

- Startup idea validation
- Pricing strategy
- MVP validation
- Investor due diligence
- Product-market fit assessment
- Revenue model design

---

## Inputs Required

- Ideal Customer Profile
- Customer interviews
- Competitor pricing
- Value proposition
- Prototype or MVP
- Market research

---

## Core Concepts

Willingness to Pay (WTP) measures the maximum amount a customer is prepared to spend to solve a problem.

WTP depends on:
- Problem severity
- ROI delivered
- Switching cost
- Available alternatives
- Budget ownership
- Perceived value

Customer actions are stronger evidence than stated opinions.

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Validate the Problem

Confirm customers experience a meaningful and recurring problem.

### Step 2 — Estimate Delivered Value

Quantify savings in:
- Time
- Cost
- Revenue
- Risk
- Productivity

### Step 3 — Research Alternatives

Document:
- Existing solutions
- Current spending
- Competitor pricing
- Manual alternatives

### Step 4 — Test Pricing

Use:
- Pilot sales
- Pre-orders
- Paid trials
- Pricing surveys
- Landing page experiments

### Step 5 — Analyze Results

Compare willingness to pay with unit economics and profitability targets.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Problem validated
- Budget owner identified
- Existing spending confirmed
- Pricing experiments completed
- Value exceeds price
- Sustainable margins achievable

---

## Questions To Ask

- How do you solve this today?
- What does the current solution cost?
- What is the financial impact of the problem?
- What would make you switch?
- Would you pay today?

---

## Metrics

- Average Willingness to Pay
- Conversion Rate
- Trial-to-Paid Conversion
- ARPU
- Gross Margin
- CAC
- LTV

---

## Red Flags

- Positive feedback without purchase intent
- No existing budget
- Price sensitivity dominates value
- Unsustainable pricing
- Assumption-based pricing

---

## Best Practices

- Validate pricing with real purchasing behavior.
- Test multiple pricing levels.
- Focus on value delivered rather than implementation cost.
- Review pricing as the product evolves.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Asking hypothetical pricing questions.
- Competing only on low price.
- Ignoring customer ROI.
- Setting prices without experiments.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Pricing Evidence Summary
- WTP Analysis
- Supporting Evidence
- Recommended Price Range
- Risks
- Recommendation
