# Customer Interview Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Conduct structured customer interviews that validate problems, understand customer behavior, uncover unmet needs, and reduce startup risk through evidence-based learning.

---

## When To Use

- Startup idea validation
- Problem validation
- Customer discovery
- MVP planning
- Product-market fit assessment
- Investor due diligence

---

## Inputs Required

- Target ICP
- Interview objectives
- Problem hypothesis
- Interview guide
- Recording/notes
- Consent from participant

---

## Core Concepts

Customer interviews are designed to understand past behavior rather than collect opinions.

Focus on:
- Current workflow
- Existing alternatives
- Pain points
- Purchase decisions
- Frequency of the problem
- Cost of the problem

Avoid leading questions and solution pitching.

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Define Objectives

State the hypotheses to validate.

### Step 2 — Recruit Participants

Interview customers that closely match the ICP.

### Step 3 — Conduct Interviews

Ask open-ended questions.
Listen more than you speak.

### Step 4 — Analyze Findings

Identify recurring themes, objections and unmet needs.

### Step 5 — Validate

Separate evidence from assumptions and decide whether to continue, iterate or stop.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Interview matches ICP
- Open-ended questions used
- Real behavior discussed
- Pain frequency measured
- Existing solutions identified
- Findings documented

---

## Questions To Ask

- Tell me about the last time this problem occurred.
- How do you solve it today?
- What frustrates you most?
- What have you already tried?
- What would happen if nothing changed?
- Have you ever paid to solve it?

---

## Metrics

- Number of Interviews
- Problem Frequency
- Problem Severity
- Existing Solution Usage
- Willingness to Pay
- Common Themes

---

## Red Flags

- Talking more than listening
- Asking hypothetical questions
- Interviewing friends only
- Selling during interviews
- Ignoring contradictory evidence

---

## Best Practices

- Interview at least 15–30 target customers.
- Record interviews when permitted.
- Ask about past actions, not future intentions.
- Look for repeated patterns across interviews.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Leading the participant.
- Confirming existing beliefs.
- Treating compliments as validation.
- Ending interviews without follow-up questions.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Interview Summary
- Key Insights
- Pain Points
- Existing Alternatives
- Evidence Table
- Risks
- Recommendations
