# Customer Journey Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Map the complete customer experience from initial awareness to long-term advocacy. The framework identifies friction points, decision moments and improvement opportunities that increase conversion, retention and customer satisfaction.

---

## When To Use

- Startup idea validation
- Product design
- UX improvement
- Go-To-Market planning
- Customer success strategy
- Investor due diligence

---

## Inputs Required

- Ideal Customer Profile
- Buyer Personas
- Customer interviews
- Product workflow
- Analytics (if available)
- Support feedback

---

## Core Concepts

A customer journey describes every interaction a customer has with the business.

Typical stages:

- Awareness
- Consideration
- Evaluation
- Purchase
- Onboarding
- Activation
- Retention
- Expansion
- Advocacy

Each stage should identify customer goals, emotions, pain points and business opportunities.

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Define Journey Stages

Identify the major stages from discovery through advocacy.

### Step 2 — Capture Customer Goals

Document what the customer wants to accomplish at each stage.

### Step 3 — Map Touchpoints

List every interaction including website, sales, product, support and marketing.

### Step 4 — Identify Friction

Highlight delays, confusion, drop-off points and unmet expectations.

### Step 5 — Improve Experience

Recommend changes that simplify the journey and improve customer outcomes.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Journey covers the complete lifecycle
- Customer goals documented
- Touchpoints identified
- Pain points validated
- Success metrics defined
- Improvement opportunities prioritized

---

## Questions To Ask

- How does the customer first discover the product?
- What information is required before buying?
- Where do customers abandon the journey?
- What creates delight?
- What drives long-term loyalty?

---

## Metrics

- Awareness Rate
- Conversion Rate
- Activation Rate
- Time to Value
- Retention Rate
- Churn Rate
- NPS
- Referral Rate

---

## Red Flags

- Complex onboarding
- High abandonment
- Inconsistent messaging
- Poor support experience
- No retention strategy

---

## Best Practices

- Base the journey on customer research.
- Focus on reducing friction.
- Optimize the highest-impact stages first.
- Continuously update the journey.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Designing from the company's perspective.
- Ignoring post-purchase experience.
- Assuming every customer follows the same path.
- Measuring only acquisition.
