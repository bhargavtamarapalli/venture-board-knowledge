# Go-To-Market Strategy Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Develop a structured strategy for acquiring, converting and retaining customers while achieving sustainable growth. This framework aligns product, marketing, sales and customer success to maximize market adoption and business performance.

---

## When To Use

- Startup idea validation
- Product launch
- Market expansion
- Investor due diligence
- Revenue planning
- Growth strategy

---

## Inputs Required

- Ideal Customer Profile
- Buyer Personas
- Product positioning
- Market research
- Competitor analysis
- Pricing strategy
- Budget and resources

---

## Core Concepts

A Go-To-Market (GTM) strategy defines how a startup reaches its target customers, communicates value and generates revenue.

A successful GTM strategy aligns:

- Target customer
- Value proposition
- Acquisition channels
- Sales process
- Pricing
- Customer success

The objective is to achieve repeatable and scalable customer acquisition.

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Define Target Market

Identify the primary customer segment and beachhead market.

### Step 2 — Define Value Proposition

Clearly articulate the problem solved and measurable customer benefits.

### Step 3 — Select Acquisition Channels

Evaluate:

- Organic Search
- Content Marketing
- Social Media
- Paid Advertising
- Partnerships
- Direct Sales
- Product-Led Growth
- Community

### Step 4 — Define Sales Process

Map the customer journey from awareness to purchase.

### Step 5 — Launch and Measure

Track acquisition, activation, conversion, retention and revenue.

Iterate continuously using customer feedback and performance data.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Target market validated
- Customer value proposition defined
- Acquisition channels selected
- Sales process documented
- Metrics established
- Budget realistic

---

## Questions To Ask

- Who is the first customer?
- Which acquisition channel scales best?
- What creates competitive advantage?
- How will success be measured?
- What assumptions require validation?

---

## Metrics

- CAC
- Conversion Rate
- Activation Rate
- Sales Cycle Length
- Customer Retention
- LTV
- Payback Period
- Revenue Growth

---

## Red Flags

- Targeting multiple markets simultaneously
- No repeatable acquisition strategy
- Weak product positioning
- High CAC without retention
- Undefined sales process

---

## Best Practices

- Focus on one beachhead market.
- Validate channels before scaling.
- Align GTM with customer evidence.
- Continuously refine using metrics.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Expanding too quickly.
- Ignoring unit economics.
- Choosing channels based on assumptions.
- Measuring only acquisition.
