# Go-To-Market Marketing Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Define a structured marketing strategy that creates awareness, generates qualified demand, converts prospects into customers and supports sustainable business growth. The framework aligns marketing activities with customer needs, product positioning and measurable business outcomes.

---

## When To Use

- Startup launch
- Go-To-Market execution
- Demand generation
- Brand development
- Growth planning
- Investor due diligence

---

## Inputs Required

- Ideal Customer Profile
- Buyer Personas
- Positioning statement
- Value proposition
- Marketing budget
- Competitive analysis

---

## Core Concepts

Marketing should communicate value to the right audience through the right channel at the right time.

An effective marketing strategy aligns:

- Audience
- Message
- Channel
- Content
- Conversion
- Measurement

Focus on repeatable acquisition rather than vanity exposure.

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Define Marketing Objectives

Examples:
- Brand awareness
- Lead generation
- Product adoption
- Revenue growth

### Step 2 — Develop Core Messaging

Communicate:
- Customer problem
- Desired outcome
- Unique value
- Proof

### Step 3 — Select Marketing Channels

Evaluate:
- SEO
- Content Marketing
- Social Media
- Email
- Paid Advertising
- Partnerships
- Communities
- Events

### Step 4 — Create Content Strategy

Map content to each buying stage:
- Awareness
- Consideration
- Decision
- Retention

### Step 5 — Measure & Optimize

Review campaign performance and improve continuously.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Target audience defined
- Messaging validated
- Channels prioritized
- Budget allocated
- KPIs documented
- Attribution established

---

## Questions To Ask

- Which channel reaches the ICP most efficiently?
- What message resonates most?
- Which campaigns generate qualified leads?
- What drives conversion?
- Which activities should be stopped?

---

## Metrics

- Website Traffic
- Marketing Qualified Leads
- Conversion Rate
- CAC
- Cost per Lead
- ROAS
- Pipeline Contribution
- Revenue Attribution

---

## Red Flags

- Marketing without positioning
- Channel overload
- Vanity metrics
- No attribution
- Weak customer messaging

---

## Best Practices

- Start with one or two primary channels.
- Test messaging continuously.
- Invest in educational content.
- Measure ROI for every campaign.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Targeting everyone.
- Copying competitor campaigns.
- Ignoring customer feedback.
- Scaling before channel validation.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Marketing Strategy
- Target Audience
- Channel Plan
- Content Plan
- KPI Dashboard
- Risks
- Recommendations
