# GTM_Growth.md

# Go-To-Market Growth Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Define a repeatable growth strategy that scales customer acquisition, activation, retention and revenue through experimentation, measurement and continuous optimization.

---

## When To Use

- Startup growth planning
- Post-launch optimization
- Product-Market Fit expansion
- Investor due diligence
- Revenue scaling

---

## Inputs Required

- Product analytics
- Customer feedback
- Marketing performance
- Sales metrics
- Financial KPIs
- Growth objectives

---

## Core Concepts

Sustainable growth comes from optimizing the entire customer lifecycle rather than maximizing acquisition alone.

Primary growth stages:

- Acquisition
- Activation
- Engagement
- Retention
- Revenue
- Referral

Growth should be driven by experiments supported by measurable evidence.

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Define Growth Objective

Identify the primary business outcome.

### Step 2 — Analyze Funnel

Measure performance across every customer lifecycle stage.

### Step 3 — Identify Bottlenecks

Prioritize the highest-impact constraint.

### Step 4 — Design Experiments

Develop hypotheses, success metrics and implementation plans.

### Step 5 — Measure Results

Evaluate impact using statistically meaningful data.

### Step 6 — Scale Successful Experiments

Standardize and repeat proven growth initiatives.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- North Star Metric defined
- Funnel measured
- Bottleneck identified
- Experiment backlog maintained
- Success metrics documented
- Learning captured

---

## Questions To Ask

- Which stage limits growth?
- What experiment has the highest expected impact?
- What customer behavior predicts retention?
- Which acquisition channel scales profitably?
- How will success be measured?

---

## Metrics

- North Star Metric
- Activation Rate
- Retention Rate
- Churn Rate
- CAC
- LTV
- Viral Coefficient
- Referral Rate
- Revenue Growth

---

## Red Flags

- Growth dependent on paid acquisition only
- No experimentation process
- Weak retention
- Vanity metrics
- No measurable hypotheses

---

## Best Practices

- Improve retention before scaling acquisition.
- Prioritize one bottleneck at a time.
- Run continuous experiments.
- Make decisions using evidence.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Scaling without Product-Market Fit.
- Running too many experiments simultaneously.
- Optimizing vanity metrics.
- Ignoring customer retention.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Growth Strategy
- Funnel Analysis
- Experiment Roadmap
- KPI Dashboard
- Risks
- Recommendations
