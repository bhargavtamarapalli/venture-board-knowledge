# Go-To-Market Positioning Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Define a clear, differentiated market position that communicates who the product is for, the problem it solves, why it is better than alternatives, and why customers should choose it. Effective positioning aligns product, marketing, sales and customer success around a consistent value proposition.

---

## When To Use

- Startup idea validation
- Product launch
- Go-To-Market planning
- Brand strategy
- Messaging development
- Investor due diligence

---

## Inputs Required

- Ideal Customer Profile
- Buyer Personas
- Value Proposition Canvas
- Competitor Analysis
- Market Research
- Customer Interviews

---

## Core Concepts

Positioning determines how customers perceive the product relative to competing alternatives.

Strong positioning is:

- Customer-centric
- Problem-focused
- Differentiated
- Credible
- Memorable
- Consistent

It should answer:

- Who is the customer?
- What problem is solved?
- Why is the solution different?
- Why should customers believe it?

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Define Target Customer

Identify the primary customer segment and buying context.

### Step 2 — Define the Core Problem

State the highest-priority problem the customer wants to solve.

### Step 3 — Identify Differentiation

Document the unique capabilities that competitors cannot easily match.

### Step 4 — Craft Positioning Statement

Include:

- Target customer
- Category
- Primary benefit
- Differentiator
- Proof

### Step 5 — Validate Positioning

Test messaging with customers through interviews, landing pages and sales conversations.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Target customer clearly defined
- Problem validated
- Differentiation supported by evidence
- Messaging easy to understand
- Value proposition compelling
- Positioning consistent across channels

---

## Questions To Ask

- Why should customers switch?
- What makes the product memorable?
- Which benefit matters most?
- Can competitors make the same claim?
- Is the positioning supported by evidence?

---

## Metrics

- Brand Recall
- Conversion Rate
- Win Rate
- Customer Preference
- Message Recall
- Sales Cycle Length

---

## Red Flags

- Generic messaging
- No differentiation
- Feature-heavy messaging
- Unsupported claims
- Multiple conflicting value propositions

---

## Best Practices

- Focus on customer outcomes.
- Keep messaging simple.
- Validate positioning continuously.
- Reinforce positioning across every customer touchpoint.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Trying to appeal to everyone.
- Confusing features with positioning.
- Ignoring customer perception.
- Frequently changing core messaging.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Positioning Statement
- Target Customer
- Value Proposition
- Key Differentiators
- Supporting Evidence
- Risks
- Recommendations
