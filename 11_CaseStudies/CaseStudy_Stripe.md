# Case Study: Stripe

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Analyze Stripe's evolution from a developer-focused payment API into a global financial infrastructure platform. This case study highlights the strategic decisions, execution principles and competitive advantages that made Stripe one of the world's most valuable private technology companies.

---

## Company Snapshot

**Company:** Stripe

**Founded:** 2010

**Industry:** Financial Infrastructure / Payments

**Business Model:** Transaction-based platform charging fees for payment processing and value-added financial services.

---

## Problem

Accepting online payments was historically slow, technically complex and fragmented across banks, gateways and payment providers.

Developers and startups needed a simple way to integrate payments without negotiating multiple banking relationships.

---

## Initial Validation

Stripe launched with a narrow focus:

- Simple payment APIs
- Excellent developer experience
- Fast onboarding
- Minimal documentation friction

Early adopters integrated the product quickly, validating strong demand before expanding into broader financial services.

---

## Product Strategy

The MVP focused on one core capability:

- Accept online payments with minimal code.

Early product priorities included:

- REST APIs
- SDKs
- Clear documentation
- Reliable payment processing
- Developer dashboard

Additional financial products were introduced only after strong adoption.

---

## Go-To-Market Strategy

Stripe targeted developers first.

Growth drivers included:

- Product-led adoption
- Exceptional documentation
- Developer communities
- Word-of-mouth referrals
- Startup ecosystem partnerships

The product sold itself through ease of implementation.

---

## Business Model

Primary revenue source:

- Percentage fee per successful transaction.

Expansion opportunities included:

- Billing
- Connect
- Atlas
- Issuing
- Treasury
- Fraud prevention

The model increased customer lifetime value through platform expansion.

---

## Competitive Advantages

Stripe developed multiple durable moats:

- Superior developer experience
- Extensive API ecosystem
- Global payment infrastructure
- High switching costs
- Ecosystem integrations
- Brand trust
- Continuous product expansion

These advantages compounded as more businesses adopted the platform.

---

## Risks Faced

- Regulatory complexity
- Fraud
- Payment reliability
- Intense competition
- Global compliance
- Enterprise sales execution

Continuous investment in infrastructure and compliance reduced these risks.

---

## Key Metrics

Examples of important metrics:

- Payment Volume
- Active Businesses
- API Reliability
- Transaction Success Rate
- Gross Payment Volume
- Revenue Growth
- Net Revenue Retention

---

## Lessons for Startup Evaluation

- Solve one painful problem exceptionally well.
- Prioritize customer experience over feature quantity.
- Build for developers if they influence purchasing.
- Expand only after establishing a trusted core platform.
- Increase customer value through adjacent products.
- Reliability becomes a competitive moat.

---

## Evaluation Questions

- Does the product eliminate significant customer friction?
- Is onboarding dramatically simpler than competitors?
- Can expansion increase customer lifetime value?
- Are switching costs increasing over time?
- Does the platform become more valuable as customers grow?
