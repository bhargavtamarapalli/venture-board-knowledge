# Blue Ocean Strategy Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Evaluate whether a startup can create uncontested market space instead of competing directly in saturated markets.

---

## When To Use

- Startup idea validation
- Product differentiation
- Strategic planning
- Market positioning
- Innovation workshops

---

## Inputs Required

- Startup idea
- Target customers
- Existing alternatives
- Competitive landscape

---

## Core Concepts

A **Red Ocean** competes in existing markets through price and incremental improvements.

A **Blue Ocean** creates new value by solving problems differently and making competition less relevant.

---

## Four Actions Framework

### Eliminate
Remove industry assumptions or features that no longer create value.

### Reduce
Reduce investments in low-value activities or features.

### Raise
Increase factors customers genuinely value.

### Create
Introduce new value that competitors do not offer.

---

## Strategy Canvas

Compare:
- Your startup
- Direct competitors
- Alternative solutions

Evaluate value delivered across major customer buying factors.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Is the value proposition unique?
- Is price competition avoidable?
- Are customers underserved?
- Is differentiation difficult to copy?
- Does the idea create new demand?

---

## Questions To Ask

- Which assumptions can be eliminated?
- What do customers tolerate but dislike?
- What new value can be created?
- Can a non-customer become a customer?
- Why would customers switch?

---

## Metrics

- Differentiation score
- Customer value increase
- Price premium potential
- Switching rate
- Competitive overlap
- Market expansion potential

---

## Red Flags

- Competing only on price
- Feature parity with incumbents
- No meaningful differentiation
- Easy to replicate innovation
- Existing market saturation

---

## Best Practices

- Focus on customer outcomes instead of features.
- Target underserved or ignored customer segments.
- Combine with JTBD and Value Proposition Canvas.
- Validate demand before building.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Confusing novelty with value.
- Creating features instead of solving problems.
- Ignoring existing customer behavior.
- Assuming competitors will not respond.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Red Ocean Assessment
- Blue Ocean Opportunities
- ERRC (Eliminate, Reduce, Raise, Create) Grid
- Strategy Canvas Summary
- Differentiation Assessment
- Recommended Strategic Actions
