# Market Validation Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Validate that a market has sufficient demand before significant investment. This framework focuses on gathering objective evidence that customers have a real, frequent, and valuable problem worth solving.

---

## When To Use

- Startup idea validation
- MVP planning
- Investor due diligence
- Product-market fit assessment
- Market entry decisions

---

## Inputs Required

- Target customer segment
- Problem statement
- Customer interview data
- Landing page or waitlist metrics
- Survey results
- Competitor observations

---

## Core Concepts

Market validation reduces execution risk by replacing assumptions with evidence.

Evidence should come from real customer behavior rather than opinions.

Validation progresses through four stages:

### Problem Validation
Confirm customers experience the problem.

### Solution Validation
Confirm customers believe the proposed solution is valuable.

### Demand Validation
Confirm measurable market interest through actions.

### Commercial Validation
Confirm willingness to pay.

---

## Framework

1. Define the hypothesis.
2. Identify the target customer.
3. Conduct customer interviews.
4. Test demand using low-cost experiments.
5. Measure willingness to pay.
6. Analyze findings.
7. Decide: Proceed, Iterate, or Stop.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Problem frequently occurs
- Customer actively seeks alternatives
- Evidence from real users
- Demand demonstrated through actions
- Pricing interest validated
- Assumptions documented

---

## Questions To Ask

- How is the problem solved today?
- How often does it occur?
- What is the cost of ignoring it?
- Would customers pay for a better solution?
- What evidence supports demand?

---

## Metrics

- Customer Interviews
- Waitlist Signups
- Landing Page Conversion
- Prototype Usage
- Willingness to Pay
- Retention Intent
- Customer Acquisition Cost

---

## Red Flags

- No customer interviews
- Feedback only from friends
- Vanity metrics
- No willingness-to-pay evidence
- Building before validating

---

## Best Practices

- Validate the problem before the solution.
- Prioritize observed behavior over stated opinions.
- Run multiple experiments.
- Record every assumption.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Asking leading questions.
- Treating compliments as validation.
- Ignoring negative feedback.
- Scaling without evidence.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Validation Summary
- Evidence Collected
- Key Assumptions
- Experiment Results
- Risks
- Confidence Level
- Recommendation
