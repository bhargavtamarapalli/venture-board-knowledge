# Market Sizing Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Provide a structured methodology for estimating the realistic size of a startup opportunity. This framework helps founders and investors quantify demand, validate commercial potential, and support financial projections using defensible assumptions.

---

## When To Use

- Startup idea validation
- Investor due diligence
- Financial planning
- Business model evaluation
- Market opportunity analysis
- Go-To-Market planning

---

## Inputs Required

- Target customer definition
- Geographic scope
- Industry reports
- Public datasets
- Pricing assumptions
- Customer interview insights

---

## Core Concepts

Market sizing should be evidence-based and transparent.

Three primary approaches are recommended:

### Top-Down

Start with industry-level data and progressively narrow the market.

Best for:
- Early validation
- Industry overview

Limitation:
- Often overestimates opportunity.

---

### Bottom-Up

Estimate demand using customer counts and expected spending.

Formula:

Market Size = Number of Customers × Average Annual Revenue per Customer

Preferred for investor-grade analysis.

---

### Value Theory

Estimate market value based on the economic value created for customers.

Useful for disruptive products with limited historical market data.

---

## Framework

1. Define the customer segment.
2. Select the sizing methodology.
3. Document assumptions.
4. Calculate market opportunity.
5. Validate using independent sources.
6. Perform sensitivity analysis.
7. Compare against competitor estimates.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Customer clearly defined
- Assumptions documented
- Bottom-up analysis completed
- Multiple sources compared
- Pricing validated
- Growth assumptions justified

---

## Questions To Ask

- Who is the buyer?
- How many buyers exist?
- How often will they purchase?
- What is annual spending?
- What assumptions drive the estimate?

---

## Metrics

- Addressable Customers
- Annual Revenue per Customer
- Market Size
- CAGR
- Market Penetration
- Revenue Potential

---

## Red Flags

- Unsupported assumptions
- Single data source
- Unrealistic penetration rates
- No sensitivity analysis
- Confusing TAM with obtainable revenue

---

## Best Practices

- Prefer bottom-up estimates.
- Cross-check multiple sources.
- State every assumption explicitly.
- Update estimates with new evidence.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Double counting customers.
- Ignoring geographic limitations.
- Assuming 100% adoption.
- Using outdated industry reports.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Methodology Used
- Assumptions
- Market Size Calculation
- Sensitivity Analysis
- Risks
- Confidence Level
- Investment Assessment
