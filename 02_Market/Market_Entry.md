# Market Entry Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Provide a structured approach for selecting the most effective strategy to enter a target market while minimizing risk, optimizing resource allocation, and accelerating early traction.

---

## When To Use

- Startup launch planning
- Geographic expansion
- New customer segment entry
- Product commercialization
- Investor due diligence

---

## Inputs Required

- Target market
- Customer segment
- Competitive landscape
- Distribution channels
- Budget
- Team capabilities
- Regulatory considerations

---

## Core Concepts

Market entry determines how a startup reaches its first customers.

Common strategies include:

- Direct Sales
- Product-Led Growth (PLG)
- Channel Partners
- Marketplace Entry
- Enterprise Sales
- Community-Led Growth
- Franchising / Licensing (where applicable)

Selection depends on customer behavior, acquisition cost, sales cycle, and operational capability.

---

## Framework

1. Define target customer.
2. Evaluate market attractiveness.
3. Select entry strategy.
4. Identify distribution channels.
5. Estimate customer acquisition cost.
6. Validate through pilot launch.
7. Measure traction and iterate.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Target segment clearly defined
- Entry strategy aligned with customer buying behavior
- Distribution channels validated
- Acquisition economics understood
- Competitive response considered
- Launch risks documented

---

## Questions To Ask

- Why will customers adopt first?
- Which channel reaches them fastest?
- What barriers exist?
- What partnerships accelerate entry?
- What is the lowest-risk launch approach?

---

## Metrics

- Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
- Payback Period
- Conversion Rate
- Sales Cycle Length
- Channel Performance
- Market Penetration
- Customer Growth Rate

---

## Red Flags

- Expanding into multiple markets simultaneously
- No clear acquisition channel
- Underestimating incumbent response
- Ignoring regulatory barriers
- High CAC without retention

---

## Best Practices

- Start with a narrow beachhead market.
- Validate one acquisition channel before scaling.
- Measure unit economics early.
- Build repeatable sales and marketing processes.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Targeting everyone.
- Choosing channels based on assumptions.
- Scaling before repeatability.
- Ignoring localization requirements.

---

Produce:

- Recommended Entry Strategy
- Target Segment

- Risks
- Success Metrics
- Recommendation
