# Venture Capital Due Diligence Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Provide a comprehensive framework for evaluating whether a startup represents an investable opportunity. This framework mirrors the structured thinking used by venture capital firms to assess risk, upside potential and execution capability before making an investment decision.

---

## When To Use

- Startup idea validation
- Fundraising preparation
- Investor due diligence
- Internal investment committee reviews
- Acquisition evaluation

---

## Inputs Required

- Executive summary
- Business model
- Product strategy
- Market analysis
- Customer validation
- Financial model
- Team information
- Competitive analysis
- Risk assessment

---

## Core Concepts

Venture capital due diligence evaluates the startup across multiple dimensions to determine:

- Market attractiveness
- Founder capability
- Product differentiation
- Customer validation
- Scalability
- Financial viability
- Defensibility
- Exit potential

Investment decisions should be evidence-based rather than narrative-driven.

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Founder & Team Assessment

Evaluate:

- Founder-market fit
- Domain expertise
- Execution capability
- Leadership
- Hiring ability

---

### Step 2 — Market Assessment

Review:

- TAM / SAM / SOM
- Growth trends
- Competitive intensity
- Market timing
- Expansion potential

---

### Step 3 — Product Assessment

Evaluate:

- Product differentiation
- Customer value
- Product-Market Fit
- Roadmap
- Technology advantage

---

### Step 4 — Business Model

Review:

- Revenue model
- Pricing
- Unit economics
- Scalability
- Gross margins

---

### Step 5 — Risk Review

Assess:

- Execution risks
- Financial risks
- Market risks
- Regulatory risks
- Competitive threats

---

### Step 6 — Investment Decision

Summarize strengths, weaknesses, evidence gaps and overall investment recommendation.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Strong founding team
- Large addressable market
- Clear customer validation
- Defensible competitive position
- Sustainable unit economics
- Manageable risk profile

---

## Questions To Ask

- Why will this startup win?
- Why now?
- Can the team execute?
- What evidence supports demand?
- What could cause failure?

---

## Metrics

- TAM
- ARR
- Revenue Growth
- Gross Margin
- CAC
- LTV
- Burn Multiple
- Runway
- NRR

---

## Red Flags

- Weak founder-market fit
- No customer validation
- Small market opportunity
- Negative unit economics
- Easy competitive replication

---

## Best Practices

- Validate every claim with evidence.
- Distinguish facts from assumptions.
- Prioritize downside risk analysis.
- Update diligence as new information becomes available.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Overweighting founder charisma.
- Ignoring execution risk.
- Assuming market size guarantees success.
- Underestimating competitive response.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Investment Summary
- Due Diligence Findings
- Strengths
- Weaknesses
- Risks
- Open Questions
- Final Recommendation (INVEST / IMPROVE / DROP)
