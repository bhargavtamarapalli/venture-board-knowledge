# Investor Funding Readiness Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Assess whether a startup is prepared to raise external capital by evaluating business maturity, traction, financial health, execution capability and investment readiness. This framework identifies evidence gaps before engaging investors.

---

## When To Use

- Fundraising preparation
- Investor due diligence
- Seed or Series A readiness
- Board reviews
- Strategic planning

---

## Inputs Required

- Executive summary
- Pitch deck
- Financial model
- Business model
- Product metrics
- Customer validation
- Legal documentation
- Team information

---

## Core Concepts

Funding readiness is determined by evidence, not ambition.

A fundable startup demonstrates:

- Validated customer demand
- Scalable business model
- Strong founding team
- Healthy unit economics
- Large market opportunity
- Clear use of funds
- Credible growth plan

The objective is to minimize investor uncertainty.

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Business Readiness

Evaluate:

- Product-Market Fit
- Customer traction
- Market opportunity
- Competitive positioning

---

### Step 2 — Financial Readiness

Review:

- Revenue model
- Financial projections
- Unit economics
- Burn rate
- Runway
- Funding requirement

---

### Step 3 — Operational Readiness

Verify:

- Team capability
- Hiring plan
- Product roadmap
- Execution processes

---

### Step 4 — Legal & Governance

Confirm:

- Company incorporation
- Cap table
- IP ownership
- Regulatory compliance
- Key contracts

---

### Step 5 — Investor Readiness

Ensure:

- Investment memo prepared
- Pitch deck complete
- Data room organized
- Due diligence documents available
- Milestones clearly defined

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Customer validation complete
- Financial model credible
- Unit economics healthy
- Legal documentation prepared
- Data room organized
- Clear funding strategy defined

---

## Questions To Ask

- Why is funding required now?
- What milestones will the capital achieve?
- Is customer traction sufficient?
- What evidence reduces investor risk?
- What gaps remain before fundraising?

---

## Metrics

- ARR / MRR
- Revenue Growth
- CAC
- LTV
- Gross Margin
- Burn Rate
- Cash Runway
- Net Revenue Retention

---

## Red Flags

- Raising before validation
- Weak customer traction
- Incomplete financials
- Missing legal documentation
- Unclear use of funds

---

## Best Practices

- Raise capital for acceleration, not discovery.
- Maintain an investor-ready data room.
- Validate assumptions before fundraising.
- Prepare evidence for every major claim.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Raising too early.
- Inflated financial projections.
- Poor documentation.
- Weak milestone planning.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Funding Readiness Assessment
- Readiness Score
- Evidence Summary
- Key Gaps
- Action Plan
- Fundraising Recommendation
