# Investment Memo Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Provide a standardized framework for preparing an investment memorandum that summarizes a startup opportunity, supporting evidence, investment rationale, risks and recommendation. The memo should enable an investment committee to make an informed decision quickly using structured analysis.

---

## When To Use

- Investor due diligence
- Investment committee reviews
- Fundraising preparation
- Internal startup evaluations
- Acquisition assessments

---

## Inputs Required

- Executive summary
- Market analysis
- Customer validation
- Product strategy
- Business model
- Financial model
- Risk assessment
- Competitive analysis

---

## Core Concepts

An investment memo synthesizes evidence into a concise decision document.

Every statement should be supported by facts, validated assumptions or clearly identified uncertainties.

The memo should communicate:

- Opportunity
- Evidence
- Risks
- Investment thesis
- Recommendation

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Executive Summary

Summarize the startup, market, traction and funding request.

---

### Step 2 — Investment Thesis

Explain why the opportunity is attractive.

Include:

- Market timing
- Customer need
- Competitive advantage
- Founder capability

---

### Step 3 — Business Analysis

Evaluate:

- Product
- Market
- Customers
- Competition
- Business model
- Financial viability

---

### Step 4 — Risk Assessment

Identify:

- Market risks
- Product risks
- Execution risks
- Financial risks
- Regulatory risks

Document mitigation strategies.

---

### Step 5 — Investment Recommendation

Provide one recommendation:

- INVEST
- IMPROVE
- DROP

Support the recommendation with evidence.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Executive summary complete
- Evidence cited
- Risks documented
- Investment thesis clear
- Financial assumptions reviewed
- Recommendation justified

---

## Questions To Ask

- Why invest now?
- What evidence supports demand?
- What could invalidate the investment thesis?
- Can the team execute?
- What milestones reduce investment risk?

---

## Metrics

- TAM
- ARR
- Revenue Growth
- Gross Margin
- Burn Rate
- Runway
- CAC
- LTV

---

## Red Flags

- Unsupported assumptions
- Weak validation
- Inconsistent financials
- Missing risks
- Ambiguous recommendation

---

## Best Practices

- Keep the memo concise.
- Support conclusions with evidence.
- Highlight uncertainties.
- Separate facts from assumptions.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Writing a pitch instead of an analysis.
- Ignoring downside risks.
- Repeating raw data without insights.
- Omitting recommendation rationale.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Executive Summary
- Investment Thesis
- Key Findings
- Risks
- Recommendation
- Open Questions
- Next Steps
