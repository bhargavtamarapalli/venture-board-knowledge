# Investor Defensibility Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Evaluate whether a startup possesses sustainable competitive advantages that protect long-term value creation and reduce the risk of competitive displacement. This framework helps investors determine if the business can maintain superior performance as the market evolves.

---

## When To Use

- Investor due diligence
- Startup idea validation
- Fundraising preparation
- Strategic planning
- Competitive reviews
- Board evaluations

---

## Inputs Required

- Business model
- Competitive analysis
- Product strategy
- Customer research
- Intellectual property
- Distribution strategy
- Financial model

---

## Core Concepts

Defensibility measures how difficult it is for competitors to replicate or outperform the business.

Sources of defensibility include:

- Proprietary technology
- Data advantages
- Network effects
- Brand strength
- Distribution advantage
- Switching costs
- Community
- Operational excellence
- Regulatory advantages
- Economies of scale

Strong defensibility compounds over time and improves with customer growth.

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Identify Existing Advantages

List all structural advantages the business currently possesses.

---

### Step 2 — Classify Advantages

Categorize each advantage by type:

- Technology
- Brand
- Data
- Distribution
- Customer Lock-in
- Cost
- Community
- Regulatory

---

### Step 3 — Evaluate Strength

Assess:

- Difficulty to replicate
- Time to build
- Capital required
- Customer value
- Sustainability

---

### Step 4 — Identify Weaknesses

Highlight areas where competitors can easily respond or copy.

---

### Step 5 — Recommend Improvements

Define initiatives that strengthen long-term defensibility.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Structural advantages identified
- Replication difficulty assessed
- Customer lock-in evaluated
- Competitive response considered
- Long-term sustainability reviewed
- Improvement roadmap documented

---

## Questions To Ask

- Why can't competitors copy this?
- Does the advantage improve with scale?
- What creates switching costs?
- Can incumbents neutralize the advantage?
- Will defensibility strengthen over time?

---

## Metrics

- Customer Retention
- Switching Cost
- Network Growth
- Brand Awareness
- Gross Margin
- Market Share
- Customer Lifetime Value
- Expansion Revenue

---

## Red Flags

- Feature-based differentiation only
- No switching costs
- Commodity product
- Weak customer loyalty
- Temporary technology advantage

---

## Best Practices

- Build multiple reinforcing moats.
- Strengthen customer lock-in.
- Continuously invest in differentiation.
- Review defensibility quarterly.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Confusing innovation with defensibility.
- Ignoring distribution advantages.
- Assuming first-mover advantage lasts forever.
- Underestimating incumbent reactions.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Defensibility Assessment
- Competitive Advantages
- Weaknesses
- Improvement Opportunities
- Risk Assessment
- Overall Defensibility Rating
