# Investor Moat Evaluation Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Evaluate the strength, durability and scalability of a startup's competitive moat from an investor's perspective. This framework determines whether the company can defend market share, maintain pricing power and sustain long-term value creation.

---

## When To Use

- Investor due diligence
- Startup idea validation
- Fundraising preparation
- Board reviews
- Strategic planning
- Acquisition evaluations

---

## Inputs Required

- Business model
- Product strategy
- Competitive analysis
- Customer research
- Financial model
- Technology overview
- Distribution strategy

---

## Core Concepts

A moat is a durable structural advantage that becomes stronger over time and makes competitive replication difficult.

Common moat types include:

- Network Effects
- Proprietary Technology
- Data Advantage
- Brand
- Switching Costs
- Distribution
- Economies of Scale
- Community
- Regulatory Barriers
- Operational Excellence

The strongest businesses possess multiple reinforcing moats.

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Identify Existing Moats

List every current source of competitive advantage.

---

### Step 2 — Evaluate Durability

Assess:

- Replication difficulty
- Time to copy
- Capital required
- Customer dependence
- Sustainability

---

### Step 3 — Assess Scalability

Determine whether each moat strengthens as the business grows.

---

### Step 4 — Evaluate Competitive Response

Analyze how incumbents and new entrants could respond.

---

### Step 5 — Score Overall Moat

Rate each moat using:

- Strength
- Sustainability
- Customer Impact
- Scalability
- Defensibility

Provide an overall investment assessment.

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Moats clearly identified
- Evidence supports each moat
- Customer lock-in evaluated
- Competitive response analyzed
- Scalability assessed
- Weaknesses documented

---

## Questions To Ask

- Why can't competitors replicate this?
- Does the moat compound over time?
- Does customer growth strengthen the moat?
- What is the biggest vulnerability?
- Which moat creates pricing power?

---

## Metrics

- Customer Retention
- Switching Cost
- Gross Margin
- Expansion Revenue
- Network Growth
- Market Share
- Brand Awareness
- Customer Lifetime Value

---

## Red Flags

- No durable competitive advantage
- Feature-based differentiation only
- Commodity pricing
- Weak customer retention
- Easy replication

---

## Best Practices

- Build multiple complementary moats.
- Continuously strengthen defensibility.
- Validate moat claims using evidence.
- Review moat evolution regularly.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Assuming technology alone is a moat.
- Ignoring distribution.
- Overestimating first-mover advantage.
- Confusing innovation with defensibility.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Moat Assessment
- Moat Classification
- Strength Ratings
- Competitive Threats
- Improvement Opportunities
- Overall Investment Recommendation
