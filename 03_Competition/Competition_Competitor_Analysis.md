# Competitor Analysis Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Systematically evaluate direct, indirect and substitute competitors to understand market dynamics, identify differentiation opportunities and assess competitive risk. This framework enables evidence-based positioning and investor-grade due diligence.

---

## When To Use

- Startup idea validation
- Investor due diligence
- Product strategy
- Market entry planning
- Go-To-Market planning
- Pricing strategy
- Moat evaluation

---

## Inputs Required

- Startup idea
- Target customer segment
- Geographic market
- Competitor list
- Public pricing information
- Product feature comparison
- Customer reviews
- Market research

---

## Core Concepts

Competitor analysis is not about copying competitors.

Its objective is to understand:

- Customer alternatives
- Market expectations
- Competitive gaps
- Differentiation opportunities
- Execution risks

Classify every competitor into one of three categories:

### Direct Competitors

Solve the same problem for the same customer.

### Indirect Competitors

Solve the same problem differently.

### Substitute Solutions

Customers use another process, tool or manual workflow instead of purchasing software.

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Identify Competitors

List:

- Direct competitors
- Indirect competitors
- Substitute solutions

---

### Step 2 — Profile Each Competitor

Capture:

- Company
- Target customers
- Core value proposition
- Pricing model
- Strengths
- Weaknesses
- Funding (via live research)
- Market positioning

---

### Step 3 — Compare Capabilities

Evaluate:

- Features
- User experience
- Pricing
- Distribution
- Customer support
- Brand
- Integrations
- Technology

---

### Step 4 — Identify Market Gaps

Look for:

- Underserved customers
- Missing features
- Poor customer experience
- Slow innovation
- High prices
- Operational inefficiencies

---

### Step 5 — Competitive Risk Assessment

Evaluate:

- Ease of imitation
- Incumbent response
- Switching costs
- Customer loyalty
- Distribution advantages

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Direct competitors identified
- Substitute solutions identified
- Competitive advantages documented
- Weaknesses validated
- Differentiation clearly articulated
- Market gaps supported by evidence

---

## Questions To Ask

- Why do customers choose competitors?
- Why do customers leave competitors?
- What problem remains unsolved?
- What can be done significantly better?
- What prevents incumbents from copying the idea?

---

## Metrics

- Market Share
- Customer Ratings
- Feature Coverage
- Pricing Index
- Switching Cost
- Brand Strength
- Time to Value
- Product Differentiation

---

## Red Flags

- No identifiable competitors
- Competing solely on price
- No clear differentiation
- Easy replication
- Commodity market
- Ignoring substitute products

---

## Best Practices

- Use live web research for current competitor information.
- Focus on customer outcomes rather than feature counts.
- Validate assumptions through customer interviews.
- Update analysis regularly.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Comparing only features.
- Ignoring substitute solutions.
- Assuming competitors will not react.
- Copying incumbents instead of differentiating.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Competitor Matrix
- Direct Competitors
- Indirect Competitors
- Substitute Solutions
- Comparative Analysis
- Market Gaps
- Strategic Recommendations
- Competitive Risk Assessment
