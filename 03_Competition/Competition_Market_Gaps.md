# Market Gaps Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Identify underserved customer needs, unmet market opportunities, and competitive weaknesses that can be transformed into sustainable startup advantages. This framework helps founders discover opportunities where value creation exceeds existing market offerings.

---

## When To Use

- Startup idea validation
- New product discovery
- Market opportunity assessment
- Competitive strategy
- Investor due diligence
- Innovation workshops

---

## Inputs Required

- Competitor analysis
- Customer interviews
- Market research
- Customer reviews
- Industry reports
- Product comparisons

---

## Core Concepts

A market gap exists when customer expectations exceed the value currently delivered by available solutions.

Market gaps generally emerge from:

- Poor customer experience
- High prices
- Missing functionality
- Slow innovation
- Underserved customer segments
- Operational inefficiencies

The objective is to identify gaps that are meaningful, profitable and defensible.

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Analyze Existing Solutions

Evaluate:

- Product capabilities
- Customer satisfaction
- Pricing
- Distribution
- Customer support

---

### Step 2 — Identify Customer Frustrations

Collect evidence from:

- Reviews
- Interviews
- Support forums
- Social media
- Community discussions

---

### Step 3 — Categorize Gaps

Types:

- Feature Gaps
- Experience Gaps
- Pricing Gaps
- Accessibility Gaps
- Technology Gaps
- Service Gaps

---

### Step 4 — Evaluate Opportunity

Assess:

- Market size
- Customer demand
- Competitive intensity
- Technical feasibility
- Revenue potential

---

### Step 5 — Prioritize

Rank opportunities using:

- Customer impact
- Business impact
- Ease of execution
- Strategic alignment

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Gap supported by customer evidence
- Existing alternatives inadequate
- Market sufficiently large
- Opportunity economically viable
- Difficult for competitors to eliminate quickly

---

## Questions To Ask

- What frustrates customers most?
- Which needs remain unsolved?
- Why haven't competitors addressed this gap?
- Can the gap support a sustainable business?
- Is the opportunity scalable?

---

## Metrics

- Customer Satisfaction
- NPS
- Complaint Frequency
- Switching Rate
- Market Growth
- Revenue Potential
- Competitive Density

---

## Red Flags

- Solving insignificant problems
- Gaps based on assumptions
- Extremely small niche
- Easy competitor replication
- Low willingness to pay

---

## Best Practices

- Validate every gap with customer evidence.
- Focus on high-frequency problems.
- Combine with JTBD and Value Proposition Canvas.
- Continuously monitor changing customer needs.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Confusing features with opportunities.
- Ignoring substitute solutions.
- Chasing trends without demand.
- Building before validating.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Identified Market Gaps
- Opportunity Assessment
- Supporting Evidence
- Strategic Recommendations
- Risks
- Priority Ranking
