# Competitive Positioning Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Define how a startup should position itself relative to competitors to create a clear, defensible and differentiated value proposition. This framework evaluates positioning from the customer's perspective and ensures messaging aligns with real competitive advantages.

---

## When To Use

- Startup idea validation
- Product positioning
- Go-To-Market planning
- Brand strategy
- Investor due diligence
- Marketing strategy

---

## Inputs Required

- Target customer segment
- Competitor analysis
- Value proposition
- Customer interviews
- Product capabilities
- Market research

---

## Core Concepts

Competitive positioning answers one question:

**"Why should the customer choose this product instead of the alternatives?"**

Strong positioning is:

- Customer-centric
- Easy to understand
- Difficult to copy
- Supported by evidence

Positioning is built on differentiation rather than feature parity.

---

## Framework

### 1. Define Target Customer

Identify the primary customer segment and their highest-priority problem.

### 2. Identify Alternatives

List direct competitors, indirect competitors and substitute solutions.

### 3. Define Differentiators

Identify advantages in:
- Customer experience
- Product capability
- Speed
- Cost
- Convenience
- Technology
- Distribution

### 4. Validate Customer Perception

Confirm customers recognize and value the proposed differentiation.

### 5. Positioning Statement

Create a concise positioning statement that communicates:
- Target customer
- Problem solved
- Key benefit
- Primary differentiator

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Customer clearly defined
- Competitors identified
- Differentiation evidence-based
- Message simple and memorable
- Position supported by product capabilities
- Difficult for competitors to replicate

---

## Questions To Ask

- Why should customers switch?
- What is uniquely valuable?
- What is difficult to copy?
- Does the positioning align with customer priorities?
- Is differentiation sustainable?

---

## Metrics

- Brand Recall
- Win Rate
- Customer Preference
- Switching Rate
- NPS
- Conversion Rate

---

## Red Flags

- Generic messaging
- Competing only on price
- Feature parity
- No meaningful differentiation
- Unsupported claims

---

## Best Practices

- Focus on customer outcomes.
- Validate positioning with interviews.
- Keep messaging consistent.
- Update positioning as markets evolve.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Targeting everyone.
- Confusing features with positioning.
- Ignoring customer perception.
- Overcomplicating messaging.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Positioning Statement
- Competitor Comparison
- Key Differentiators
- Customer Value Proposition
- Strategic Recommendations
- Risks
