# Pricing Analysis Framework

**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Evaluate competitor pricing strategies, customer willingness to pay, and pricing opportunities to determine an optimal pricing model that maximizes customer value, profitability, and competitive positioning.

---

## When To Use

- Startup idea validation
- Pricing strategy development
- Investor due diligence
- Go-To-Market planning
- Product launch
- Revenue optimization

---

## Inputs Required

- Competitor pricing
- Product positioning
- Target customer segment
- Business model
- Customer willingness-to-pay research
- Market research

---

## Core Concepts

Pricing should reflect customer value rather than development cost.

The objective is to identify:

- Customer willingness to pay
- Competitor pricing benchmarks
- Sustainable margins
- Pricing differentiation
- Monetization opportunities

---

## Framework

### Step 1 — Identify Pricing Models

Examples:

- Subscription
- One-time purchase
- Usage-based
- Freemium
- Tiered pricing
- Enterprise contracts

---

### Step 2 — Analyze Competitors

Capture:

- Price
- Billing frequency
- Free plan
- Trial availability
- Enterprise offering
- Premium features

---

### Step 3 — Evaluate Customer Value

Assess:

- ROI delivered
- Cost savings
- Time savings
- Revenue increase
- Risk reduction

---

### Step 4 — Estimate Unit Economics

Evaluate:

- Gross Margin
- CAC
- LTV
- Payback Period
- Average Revenue per Customer

---

### Step 5 — Pricing Recommendation

Recommend:

- Pricing model
- Entry price
- Expansion opportunities
- Risks

---

## Evaluation Checklist

- Pricing aligned with value
- Competitor benchmark completed
- Willingness-to-pay validated
- Healthy margins achievable
- Pricing supports growth

---

## Questions To Ask

- Why will customers pay?
- How does pricing compare with competitors?
- Which pricing model scales best?
- Can pricing increase over time?
- Is pricing easy to understand?

---

## Metrics

- ARPU
- MRR
- ARR
- Gross Margin
- CAC
- LTV
- Payback Period
- Conversion Rate

---

## Red Flags

- Competing only on low price
- Unsustainable margins
- No pricing validation
- Hidden pricing complexity
- Ignoring customer value

---

## Best Practices

- Price according to value delivered.
- Validate willingness to pay before launch.
- Keep pricing transparent.
- Review pricing regularly.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Pricing below value.
- Copying competitors blindly.
- Ignoring unit economics.
- Offering too many pricing plans.

---

## Output Format

Produce:

- Competitor Pricing Matrix
- Pricing Model Comparison
- Value Assessment
- Recommended Pricing Strategy
- Risks
- Strategic Recommendations
